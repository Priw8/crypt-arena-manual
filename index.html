1. The basics

Crypt Arena is a PvP mod - this means that instead of going through the floors of the crypt normally, you fight other players! Use numerous familiar items, and some new ones too, to your advantage to come out on top!

At the beginning of the game, all players are sent to their starting rooms, where they can choose 3 items - one from each set of 3 random ones. After that, they are brought to the arena where they fight it out and can find more randomly spawned items.

It is recommended to use the Dynamic Sprites (DynChar) mod alongside Crypt Arena, since it allows seeing what kind of items other players have. However, I decided to keep it optional (unlike Player Hearts, which is set as a depencency).

2. Game modes

The mod features 2 different game modes - last man standing is rather simple, and as the name suggests, the player that manages to outlast all other players gets the win.

Deathmatch mode has a bit more to it - shortly after getting killed, players respawn in their starting rooms. Here, the winner is the player that gets the most points, which are awarded for killing others. More details of how the points are awarded can be found in the Detailed mechanics section.

You can also play in teams for both of these game modes! Teams can be enabled within mod settings of Crypt Arena.

3. Arenas

Games obviously do not happen on regular maps, since they aren't suitable for PvP for the most part. Crypt Arena uses LibLevelGen to implement its own procedural level generation for arena maps. 2 generators are currently available, and can be selected from LibLevelGen settings:

  • Zone Bleed - the default generator, which generates maps that sort of combine 2 zones.
  • Shops - generates a map which, uh, has 4 shops. Mostly a meme.
Using custom dungeons as arenas is also possible, but Crypt Arena expects certain marker entities to be present on them and will not work properly if they are missing. Proper tools to make your own custom dungeon arenas will be released in near future.

4. Items

Many items you know from regular Crypt of the Necrodancer can be found in Crypt Arena as well, although some were modified a bit for balancing reasons. Certain new items also appear - this section explains all the changes and new items!

Cookies
Modified to come in stacks of 3 instead of 6.
Electricity tomes
Temporarily grant electricity effect for a short time after use. The effect stacks with other sources with electricity, such as the wire or arc dagger.
Wind tomes
Use these to push away nearby enemies and players. If you push a player into a trap or an enemy that kills them, you get the point for the kill in deathmatch mode! You can even use them to "kick" bombs - isn't that neat?
Fireball items
Fireball items received some balance modifications to be more suitable for PvP - they now have limited range (3 tiles in both horizontal directions), and normal fireball deals 2 damage while greater fireball deals 4. Despite the reduced damage, regular fireball can still be used to open crates.
Pulse items
Pulse items also received balance modifications - similar to fireballs, regular pulse deals 2 damage and greater pulse deals 4. Both versions of the spell are now also piercing. Like fireballs, pulse can still be used to open crates, even with 2 damage.
Shield items
Duration of the shield has been halved for both normal and greater shield spells.
Freeze items
Duration of the freeze has been halved for both normal and greater freeze spells.
All spells
All spells had their cooldowns and blood cast costs reduced to values more suitable for PvP.
Grenade charm
The damage buff has been removed, so grenades deal the same damage as regular bombs.
Goblin Bomber Charm
Makes you drop a lit bomb when killed.
Titanium infuse
Infuses your current weapon to titanium, if it has a titanium version. Otherwise, it can't be used.
Obsidian infuse
Infuses your current weapon to obsidian, if it has an obsidian version. Otherwise, it can't be used.
Ruby infuse
Infuses your current weapon to ruby, if it has a ruby version. Otherwise, it can't be used. Ruby weapons have 1 base damage, piercing, and heal 0.5 heart every 5 kills.
Emerald infuse
Infuses your current weapon to emerald, if it has an emerald version. Otherwise, it can't be used. Emerald weapons have 1 base damage and poison the target of the attack.
Ring of gluttony
Having this ring equipped makes consumed food heal additional 0.5 heart.
Ring of whirldwind
When you get hit with this ring equipped, damage is prevented, a greater wind spell (see wind tomes) is casted and the ring shatters.
Ring of regeneration
Ring of regeneration has been modified to provide a 0.5 heart heal every 50 beats.
Ring of peace
Ring of peace has been modiified to make the holder invisible to enemies (but not other players).
Arc dagger
Modified to provide a +2 damage boost while standing on the wire, instead of infinite damage. Using an electricity tome will also grant the damage boost.
Guns
Piercing has been removed, otherwise unchanged.
All rapiers
Instead of multiplying weapon damage by 2 on lunges, it's just increased by 1.
Battle shovel
Modified to provide a +2 damage boost after digging, instead of infinite damage.
Crown of thorns
Heal every 5 kills, no damage on pickup.

5. Arena events

As you play and fight other players, various events will happen to spice things up - they can range from changing some floor to a hazard like hot coals to spawning a powerful miniboss that may drop a nice item after being defeated. This section lists all events that can happen.

Floor modification

Changes some of the floor to a random hazard, such as hot coals or ice.

Wall modification

Places some walls of random type in some locations.

Tempo up

Temporarily increases music speed.

Tempo down

Temporarily decreases music speed.

Shop travel rune

Spawns a travel rune to a random shop somewhere.

Electricity

Grants a temporary electricity effect to all players.

Riches

Everyone receives 65 gold.

Boss

A boss spawns in the middle of the arena - after getting killed, it will drop an item.

Gigantism

Every player becomes bigger for 20 beats.

Urn

An urn containing 3 items spawns somewhere.

No return mode

Activates no return mode. Stepping into no return tiles of other players will not damage you.

Whoops, all glass

:)

Bombardment

Spawns bombs near every player. The bombs have a longer fuse than regular ones.

Confusion

Confuses every player.

6. Shops

Shop travel runes spawn in various places through the shop travel rune event.

Gold shop
A shop that sells 5 items for gold.
Blood shop
A shop that sells 5 items for health.
Transmogrifier
Allows you to transmogrify items. When transmogrifying weapons, the infuse will not change (when transmogrifying titanium, the resulting weapon is guaranteed to be titanium as well). Every player has a separate, increasing price.
Conjurer
Allows you to conjure items. Every player has a separate, increasing price.
Pawnbroker
Allows you to sell items. Unlike in the regular game, the pawnbroker is willing to pay a lot for your items! In fact, if you buy an item and then sell it, you'll gain a bit of money.

None of the shopkeepers can be killed in Crypt Arena. Gold shop and Blood shop have a chance to have a super secret shop behind the top wall, which contains an additional, cheap item.

8. Detailed mechanics

This section provides more in-depth explanations of various mechanics present in the mod.

The PvPTweaks mod

Crypt Arena has the PvPTweaks mod set as a depencency. It's used to enforce strict ordering rules for actions that otherwise would depend on the input timing. Player actions are resolved in the following order during a given beat:

  1. item/spell usage
  2. weapon attacks
  3. movement
For example, this means that you are always able to use a defensive item before you get hit by an attack from another player. It also means that if you are in attack range of someone, then they can hit you no matter which direction you move, since the attack will land before movement. This prevents really awkward rollback that could potentially happen for multiple beats in a row the order was different.

Attack penetration

Piercing and phasing attack effects have an effect not only on enemies but also other players. Piercing bypasses armor and shield spell, while phasing damage is nearly completely unavoidable (bypasses even iframes!). The only thing that can protect from phasing damage is the spawn protection (which even makes traps have no effect).

Infusing

By using items such as Titanium infuse, you can change the type of your weapon. This affects the equipped weapon specifically, the one in the holster is left unchanged. With a few exceptions, non-base weapon types can only be obtained by infusing a base weapon.

Attempting to infuse a weapon that can not be infused (such as a rifle), will not consume the infuse item - infusing will simply fail and display a flyaway.

As mentioned in the Shops section, transmogrifying a weapon keeps the current infuse. You can use this to change your weapon type without giving up the effect of the previously used item.

Poison

Poison can be applied to players and enemies through the use of emerald weapons. Target of the attack will get poisoned if the attack dealt at least 1 damage. Poisoned characters have a slight green color applied to their sprites, to indicate the status effect being active. After 3 beats of being poisoned, characters take 0.5 heart of unavoidable damage and the poison status goes away.

Consumable food can be used in order to remove the active poison status. This is the only way to avoid poison damage once the status has been applied.

When poison kills a character, the player who applied the status effect is credited for the kill.

Points in deathmatch mode

You get 1 point for every player you kill with direct damage and poison. But apart from that, when you deal damage to someone, the victim of the attack is flagged as most recently damaged by you. If they happen to die to non-player damage within 8 beats after that, you'll get the point for the kill as well.

Pushing a player by using wind spell sets the flag mentioned above as well, but only for 3 beats. It is assumed that if someone was wind spelled and then immidiately died to non-player damage, the spell was deliberately used to send the player into danger, and as such the caster should be credited for the kill.

Dying when no one recently damaged you gives you 1 negative point as a penalty.

Item tiers

There are 3 tiers of starting items, and which one is used is determined by the starting item tier setting. This tier never increases - after respawning in deathmatch mode, the same one will be used every time.

Non-starting items (the ones that spawn in chests, shops) also have 3 tiers - however, they are not the same tiers as the ones above. The initial random item tier can be customized within settings, and it's a decimal value. It slowly increases over time. Which tier is used based on the value is determined as follows:

  • 0.0 - 1.0 - only items from tier 1 can spawn
  • 1.0 - 2.0 - items from tier 1 and tier 2 can spawn. The closer the value is to 2.0, the more likely it is that the spawned item will be tier 2.
  • 2.0 - 3.0 - items from tier 2 and tier 3 can spawn. The closer the value is to 3.0, the more likely it is that the spawned item will be tier 3.
  • 3.0 - only items from tier 3 will spawn. The value does not increase further once it reaches 3.0.
There is also a secret item tier than can very rarely be used instead of the one determined by the item tier value. It contains special items like leaping. It does not contain any items that could put the finder at a big advantage.

Crates have a chance to contain a random food item instead of an item based on the item tier.

Urns have a special item pool that's only used by them.

Event tiers

Events also have tiers, and the item tier value is used to detemine the event pool that a random event should be chosen from. It's mostly to prevent certain events from happening early in the game.
